05 — Typography

Type System

Two complementary typefaces form the brand's voice: Josefin Sans for headings and display text, Outfit for body copy. Both are geometric in construction — Josefin Sans brings angular authority while Outfit provides warmth and excellent legibility.

06 — Usage Guidelines

Do's & Don'ts

Consistent application protects the brand. Follow these guidelines to maintain the integrity of the Sinai Digital identity across all touchpoints.

Do
  • Use the logo on solid white, dark navy, or off-white backgrounds
  • Maintain proportional scaling — never stretch or compress
  • Keep the minimum clear space around the mark
  • Use the monochrome (white) version on the accent orange background
  • Use the stacked lockup for display; horizontal for headers and nav
Don't
  • Place the logo on busy photographic or patterned backgrounds
  • Rotate, skew, or apply effects (drop shadows, glows, outlines)
  • Alter the logo colors or use unapproved combinations
  • Add strokes, borders, or containment shapes around the mark
  • Reproduce below 16px width in digital contexts

09 — Review Notes

Asset Quality Assessment

Observations on the v2 logo assets for review and approval.

Strengths
  • + Two-tone flame adds visual depth and sophistication over v1's single-color flame
  • + Mountain silhouette is bold, angular, and reads well at all sizes
  • + Inkscape SVG source preserves all vector data for future edits
  • + Color palette (#192938, #D38044, #944730) creates a rich, earthy warmth
  • + Multiple format delivery (SVG, JPEG, PNG, PDF)
Issues to Address
  • • White SVG (white-original.svg) uses fill-opacity:0 -- won't render in browsers
  • • White PNG/JPEG are low resolution (727x590) for production use
  • • SVG has A4 page-size viewBox (210x297mm) rather than tight crop to artwork
  • • No transparent PNG exports were provided (only lossy JPEG)
  • • Logo colors differ from v3.0 brand palette (see comparison below)

Consider adopting them as the updated brand palette or requesting the designer match the existing hex values.
